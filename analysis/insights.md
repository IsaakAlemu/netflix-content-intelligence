# Netflix Content Intelligence Analysis

## Dataset Overview
The goal is to explore content distribution, genre trends, actor participation, director performance, and rating patterns across Netflix titles and their associated credits using SQL-based analysis.

## Content Distribution
Movies dominate the dataset with **3758 titles**, compared to **2047 TV shows**.
This indicates that Netflix's catalog in this dataset is more heavily focused on films than serialized content.

## Top Rated Titles
Several titles achieve extremely high IMDb ratings above 9.0, indicating a group of highly acclaimed productions on the platform. The highest rated titles include "#ABtalks" and "Khawatir" with an IMDb score of 9.6, followed by globally recognized titles such as "Breaking Bad" (9.5) and "Avatar: The Last Airbender" (9.3). This highlights that both international and regional productions can achieve exceptional audience ratings.

## Actor Appearances
Some actors appear frequently across Netflix titles, suggesting strong industry presence and repeated collaborations in productions. Shah Rukh Khan leads the dataset with 30 appearances, followed by Kareena Kapoor Khan, Boman Irani, and Anupam Kher, each appearing in 25 titles. This suggests that several highly recognizable actors contribute repeatedly to the platform's content catalog.

## Director Performance
Among directors with at least three titles, several achieve strong average IMDb ratings, indicating consistent quality across multiple productions. 

Kim Won-seok leads the group with an average IMDb score of **8.70 across 3 titles**, followed by **Rajkumar Hirani (8.10 across 3 titles)** and **Bo Burnham (8.00 across 4 titles)**. 

These results highlight directors who maintain strong audience reception across multiple productions rather than relying on a single successful title.

## Genre Popularity
The dataset shows that **Drama is the most common genre with 2901 titles**, followed by **Comedy (2269)** and **Thriller (1178)**. Other frequently occurring genres include **Action (1053)**, **Romance (958)**, and **Documentation (910)**.

The genre label **"Documentation"** appears in the dataset and likely corresponds to **Documentary**, suggesting a possible data labeling inconsistency in the raw data.

This distribution suggests that Netflix heavily invests in dramatic and comedic storytelling, which are among the most widely consumed entertainment genres.
